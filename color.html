Color Theory Basics

Complementary colors (also called opposite colors) are pairs of colors that when combined, cancel eachother out and lose hue. They are directly opposite of each other on the color wheel, creating the strongest possible contrast for those two colors. The color wheel is divided into warm and cool tones. Warm colors are often said to be hues from red through yellow.Warm tones often include brown and tan colors as well. Cool colors are often said to be the hues from blue green through blue violet and most grays are included in the cool-tone category.
